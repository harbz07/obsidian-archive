> 🛡️ Phenomenological Truth Safety Card
> - [ ] **Name where I am:**
> "I'm at my desk, on my laptop, in Notion, working on phenomenology for class."
> - [ ] **Check my body:**
> Unclench jaw, drop shoulders, put both feet on the floor, take one slow breath in and out.
> - [ ] **Check the layer:**
> Am I talking about (a) the text, (b) my experience, or (c) the AI? Pick ONE layer for this note.
> - [ ] **If I feel "slippy" or too meta:**
> Stop interacting with the PDF/AI about that text for this session and write 1-2 sentences in my own words about what felt weird.
> - [ ] **Use a quick sensory reset:**
> 5-4-3-2-1 (5 things I see, 4 feel, 3 hear, 2 smell, 1 taste) or look around the room and name 5 concrete objects.
> - [ ] **Anchor statement:**
> "I'm [your name], it's [day/time in Hawai'i], this is just study and reflection, I am safe and can close this tab anytime."
> - [ ] **Hard stop rule:**
> If I still feel off after two grounding steps, I switch to a different task (email, dishes, or a non-phenomenology tab).

## 🔄 Intentional Task Cycling Board (Kanban)
**Instructions:**
- Use this board to manage 3–5 tasks at a time.
- Only one task in "Doing."
- Write a quick "exit note" when you switch tasks.**
**

```javascript
Board Structure:
```
```javascript
| Ready                | Doing                | Done/Paused           |
|----------------------|---------------------|-----------------------|
| [ ] Task 1           | [ ] Task X          | [ ] Task Y            |
| [ ] Task 2           |                     | [ ] Task Z            |
| [ ] Task 3           |                     |                       |

```
**Exit Note Template (add as a property or comment):**
> "Stopped at [point], next step is [action]."

**Intentionality Prompt (add as a property or comment):**
> "Why am I switching? (Bored, stuck, anxious, distracted?) What’s my intention for the next 15–25 minutes?
