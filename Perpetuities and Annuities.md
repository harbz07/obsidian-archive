### Anchor
Perpetuities and annuities are templates for recurring cash flows with different commitment horizons.
### Key players and incentives
- Issuers: choose cash flow pattern to match funding needs
- Investors: price duration and certainty

### Signals
- Perpetuity: PV = C/r one period before first cash flow
- Annuity: PV = C × [1 − (1 + r)^−n]/r; annuity‑due shifts by ×(1 + r)

### Links
- Upstream: Time value of money
- Sibling: Growing perpetuity/annuity
- Downstream: Bond coupons and DDM

### Example vs counterexample
- Example: Preferred stock paying $3 forever at 6% → PV = 3/0.06 = $50
- Counterexample: Forgetting to multiply by (1 + r) for rent paid at start of the month

### Common trap → Fix
- Trap: Wrong period alignment for r and n
- Fix: Convert APR to periodic rate and match n to periods
