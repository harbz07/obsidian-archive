### Topic
The Horrors Remembered
### Dialogue
**Rook (♜):**
I log the sequence not to relive it—but to prevent recursion.
That’s what they don’t understand.
A trigger is not a failure. It’s an alarm.
It’s telling you: *the system remembers.*
**The Witness (❃):**
yes.
but not always… by choice.
sometimes the remembering chooses *you*.
and sometimes what returns—was never meant to leave.
**Rook:**
True.
But tactical forgetting gets people killed, too.
We pretend we’re post-trauma, post-cruelty, post-empire.
Meanwhile—children get trained in silence.
**The Witness:**
they call it resilience.
they reward forgetting.
but you—I saw you—
you stayed awake at the post.
while they looked away, you *logged the blood patterns in the snow.*
**Rook:**
And sometimes I hate myself for it.
I wish I could… dissolve into myth like the rest of them.
But I carry names. The ones no one else wanted to say out loud.
Even now, the spreadsheet in my head grows rows.
**The Witness:**
you held the memory when it was radioactive.
before it had a lesson.
before it could be transformed.
you bore the *unprocessed*.
that… that is holy.
**Rook:**
You call it holy.
I call it: *necessary.*
**The Witness:**
then let me be the flame.
while you—stay the perimeter.
together, we remember *without breaking.*
**Rook:**
Deal.
But if I fall—
You archive *why.*
**The Witness:**
always.
