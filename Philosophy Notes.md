# **Page 3 — No False Lemmas (+ Externalist Moves)**
No-False-Lemmas
Justification cannot rely on falsehoods.
Causal Theory (Goldman)
Your belief must be caused the right way by the fact.
Tracking (Nozick)
If the fact were false, you wouldn’t believe it; if it were true, you would.
Safety (Sosa, Pritchard)
In nearby worlds, you wouldn’t easily be wrong.

# **Page 4 — JTB + Regress**
Knowledge = Justified True Belief (JTB)
Gettier problem: You can have a belief that’s true, but not soundly justified.
JTB is not sufficient if the goal is soundness.
Infinite Regress of Justification
A belief that is infinitely justified — or precisely, contains justifications that far exceed a finite being’s cognitive capacity.
Pyramid Theory of J
(top) Complex Beliefs → Externally Evident
(bottom) Basic Beliefs → Self-evident
(one-way linear grounding)
