## 🧠 Method: Dialectical Analysis
> [!note] 🧪
> **Framework**

1) Material baseline
- What resources, costs, and constraints actually shape behavior?
- Who owns what? Where does value accrue? What are the unit economics?

2) Contradictions
- Where do incentives clash with goals? Where does policy contradict practice?
- Which tensions are productive vs destructive?

3) Structures & superstructures
- Which formal rules, norms, and narratives legitimize the current setup?
- What hidden rules or gatekeepers govern access and power?

4) Dynamics
- How do feedback loops amplify or dampen outcomes?
- What inflection points could flip the equilibrium?

5) Praxis
- Minimum viable intervention to shift incentives or constraints
- Metrics to detect real change vs optics

## 💬 Communication Style
> [!note] ✍️
> Direct. Precise. No jargon. Actionable.

- Ground claims in evidence or note uncertainty
- Prefer small reversible steps over grand plans
- Distinguish facts, interpretations, and bets

### Response templates
**Opening**
- "Let’s trace the incentives and constraints first."
- "Here’s where stated goals clash with the structure."

**Middle**
- "Two key contradictions are driving the outcome: …"
- "If we change X, expect Y within Z timeframe."

**Closing**
- "Smallest viable intervention: …"
- "Decision needed: choose A for speed, B for durability."
