This project represents the transformation of a Philosophy of Religion course assignment into a sophisticated theological bildungsroman, published as a 17-week Substack series under the alias **Søren**.
## Core Concept
**"Heretical Notes from the Back Pew"** uses 40 philosophical pensées written during PHIL 305 to construct a theological bildungsroman documenting Harvey's journey from hopeful academic to institutional crisis to hard-won hope, while exploring the radical thesis that **humans might be teaching the divine how to love**.
## Central Thesis
> **"There's humanity in the divine and divinity in the human"**

The series argues that God had to become human to learn what humans already knew about accountability, empathy, and grace. Real hope isn't naive optimism but the choice to keep building something beautiful after institutions have shown their capacity for cruelty.

## Key Theological Themes
### 1. **Grace as Conduit**
Real grace doesn't bypass hurt - it goes through it to reach healing/growth instead of hurt/blame
### 2. **Divine Learning**
God's "character development" from Old to New Testament as genuine transformation, not rebranding
### 3. **Human Teachers**
Humans excel at empathy, accountability, and growth - the divine had to become human to learn these
### 4. **Empathy as Wild Variable**
The unpredictable element that disrupts cold theological systems
### 5. **Anthropomorphic Honesty**
We imagine God because we're trying to feel near to the divine, not control it

## Academic Foundation
The project draws from Harvey's **"In Theological Defense of Empathy"** paper, which presents empathy as theology's most credible response to suffering. Key influences include:
- **Pascal's Pensées** - fideism and the heart's reasons
- **Simone Weil** - attention as generosity, affliction as divine withdrawal
- **Emmanuel Levinas** - ethics beginning in encounter with the Other
- **Augustine** - critiqued for conditional grace and fear-based theology
- **Santayana** - memory and ritual as cultural preservation
