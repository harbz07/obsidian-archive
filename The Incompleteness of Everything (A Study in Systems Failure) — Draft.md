### Gödel at 2:47 AM
Any sufficiently rich formal system is incomplete. Applied to life: every framework that can model you can’t close over your truths.
### Foucault’s Interruption
Systems that “care for us” may reshape us into governable subjects — not maliciously, efficiently.
### Field Notes
1. Humans are systems of convention. Even if we could ask God about evil, there’s no satisfying answer inside the system.
2. Step back and the fabric gets messier. The beauty of being wrong is that it proves the system is working.

### Reframe
Incompleteness as a feature. Growth = operating amid unprovable premises while staying corrigible.
