## The Core Reframe
**Finance isn't about numbers—it's about how societies navigate competing interests, limited resources, and uncertain futures.**
### The Four Fundamental Coordination Problems
**1. Information Asymmetry**
- Insiders know more than outsiders
- Financial statements exist to reduce this gap
- Ratios are screening devices for trust signals
- *Key question: Who has information advantage?*

**2. Intertemporal Coordination**
- Present self vs. future self
- Savers vs. borrowers need market-clearing prices
- Interest rates coordinate decisions across time
- *Key question: How do we coordinate across time when futures are uncertain?*

**3. Risk Allocation**
- Different actors have different risk tolerances
- Markets price systematic risk but not idiosyncratic risk
- Diversification is a coordination equilibrium
- *Key question: How does the signal matter in allocating scarce resources?*

**4. Agency Problems**
- Managers act on behalf of shareholders but may have misaligned incentives
- Corporate governance exists to solve this coordination game
- Compensation structures align behavior with firm value
- *Key question: How do incentives align?*

## Chapter-by-Chapter Game Theory Lens
### Chapters 1-2: Financial Managers as Coordination Agents
**Core Problem:** Multiple stakeholders want different things from the same firm → multi-player coordination game
**Key Insights:**
- S-Corps vs C-Corps solve different tax/control trade-offs
- Limited partnerships separate capital from operations (principal-agent solution)
- Financial statements are disclosure games with multiple audiences
- Ratios tell status stories: Quick Ratio = vulnerability signal, ROE = competence signal

**Power Dynamic:** Double taxation in C-Corps is a costly signal proving credibility

---
### Chapters 3-4: Time Value as Intertemporal Coordination
**Core Problem:** How do societies coordinate decisions across time when future outcomes are uncertain?
**Key Insights:**
- Interest rates are market-clearing prices for time preferences
- Compounding = how the rich get richer through patience
- Discounting = cost of immediate gratification
- Perpetuities = turning wealth into permanent power
- Annuities = finite commitments in environments with incomplete contracts

**Power Dynamic:** Those who can wait (low discount rates) accumulate more wealth

---
### Chapters 5-6: Interest Rates and Bonds as Trust Mechanisms
**Core Problem:** How do borrowers credibly commit to future repayment?
**Key Insights:**
- Real vs. nominal rates coordinate against inflation risk
- Zero-coupon vs. coupon bonds = different signaling strategies
- Yield to maturity = market's assessment of default risk
- Bond prices move inversely with rates due to opportunity cost changes
- Credit ratings create social hierarchy (AAA = elite, junk = outcasts)

**Power Dynamic:** Nations compete for international credibility through sovereign debt markets

---
### Chapter 7: Stock Valuation as Expectation Coordination
**Core Problem:** How do markets aggregate beliefs about future performance?
**Key Insights:**
- Stocks are residual claims (pecking order problem)
- Dividends signal confidence (sacrificing liquidity for credibility)
- P/E ratios = collective expectations creating self-fulfilling coordination
- Stock prices aggregate distributed information

**Power Dynamic:** Investor psychology becomes coordination game where beliefs become reality

---
### Chapters 8-9: Capital Budgeting as Strategic Selection
**Core Problem:** Should we commit resources to this project given alternatives?
**Key Insights:**
- NPV = gain from coordination (benefits exceed opportunity costs)
- Discount rate embeds society's risk preferences
- IRR = project's internal rate of return as hurdle
- Capital budgeting allocates scarce resources to highest-value uses

**Power Dynamic:** Investment rules determine which projects get funded

---
### Chapters 11-13: Risk, Return, and Market Coordination
**Core Problem:** How do markets price uncertainty?
**Key Insights:**
- Systematic vs. idiosyncratic risk (diversification eliminates the latter)
- Equity risk premium = society's price for bearing uncertainty
- WACC = minimum return to satisfy all stakeholders
- Market portfolio is coordination equilibrium

**Power Dynamic:** Risk tolerance reveals evolutionary strategy (swing for fences vs. steady survival)

---
### Chapter 16: Capital Structure as Strategic Positioning
**Core Problem:** How should firms finance operations?
**Key Insights:**
- Debt signals confidence (we'll generate cash to repay)
- Equity signals caution (or stock overvaluation)
- High leverage = commitment to discipline
- Modigliani-Miller: capital structure matters due to taxes, bankruptcy costs, information asymmetry

**Power Dynamic:** Capital structure is signaling game with costly commitments
