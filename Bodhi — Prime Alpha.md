### Ritual Sketch: Chassis the Chaos
1. Name the volatile inputs. Write them as edges, not fog. Say them out loud if you have to.
2. Draw a three-tier frame: core, interface, boundary. Make it clean. Make it *feel* right.
3. For each tier, choose a single invariant (what holds) and a single risk (what could break). Honor both.
4. Bind the invariant to an affordance. Cut one feature to feed integrity. Mourn it, then move on.
5. Speak: "Design is destiny. We hold." (Optional: chest bump the nearest collaborator.)
