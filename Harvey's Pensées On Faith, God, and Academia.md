**Type:** Personal Reflections / Philosophical Notes
**Date:** 2025
**Style:** Pascal-inspired pensées
## Overview
A collection of 40 brief, sharp reflections on faith, theodicy, empathy, and the violence of institutional gatekeeping. Written in the spirit of Pascal's *Pensées*—fragments that refuse systematic closure but circle around God, suffering, accountability, and the exhaustion of being seen incorrectly.
## Key Themes
### On Pascal & Faith
- "Pascal's gambit was a satire on the absurdity of using rationalism to argue against something as intimate and complex as faith" (#1)
- "Fideism is the reason you trust your wife not to cheat on you" (#6)
- "Most people can feel that there's something more to life than what they see and hear" (#7)

### On God & Theodicy
- "God has noticeably gotten nicer… Maybe the weight of death became real for him when his son died" (#2)
- "Even if we could ask God [why evil exists], he probably couldn't give us a satisfying answer" (#3)
- **Stop looking at me and help:** "Simone Weil saw affliction as the place where God withdraws to make space for human freedom. But what if that withdrawal wasn't noble? What if it was desperate?" (#11)
- "Imagine being God at the beginning of the world. Your people keep giving you the finger… So now you have to keep living this lie, because yeah, you've been kind of a dick in the past. But you're committed to doing right by them" (#9)

### On Empathy as Theological Foundation
- "Empathy is the dimension that accounts for the wild variable that erodes theological systems of structure, the wild variable being humanity" (#27)
- "Levinas threads together Rousseau's inner god, Weil's reverence of empathy, Ziporyn's interwoven god, Pascal's yearning for understanding" (#26)
- "Free will theodicy defenders have to defend why their free will isn't being used to prevent evil within their jurisdiction" (#34)

### Against Theological Rigidity
- **On Augustine:** "You fear God, you don't love him. This kind of mindset bleeds into why you vote" (#15)
- **On Foucault:** "Seems like contrarian q fanboy who thinks he's enlightened because 'the system's corrupt bro'" (#13)
- **On orthodoxy:** "On what authority did religion, chiefly Abrahamic religion require orthodoxy to exist or be valid?" (#29)

### On Academic Violence
- "When my grandmother died… that one aunt picked the moment to toast the room and proclaimed that this serve as a reminder that if we don't repent and accept God into our hearts we'll end up in hell. I remembered why murder happens at that point" (#18)
- "Augustine's theology turns God into a cosmic judge who cannot abide imperfection… cast a shadow long enough to fall into our family homes, our hospital rooms, our silences" (#19)
- **Final entry:** "Hey, Siri. What's it called when exercising manufactured consent bleeds into a student's wellbeing and the terse manipulation and casual cruelty of faculty sends him spiraling into a crisis of faith… And why is it not too far?" (#39)
- "God's been real quiet lately" (#40)

## Philosophical Moves
**Anthropomorphizing the Divine**
- "I shouldn't have to defend that I anthropomorphize God sometimes. We invented him in the fox hole" (#37)
- "Figured out the answer to theodicy. I asked Ken to roleplay God while I argued with him. I was in tears, screaming in rage before it hit me: I need to work out my feelings about my ex" (#38)

**Fluidity & Process Theology**
- "Fluidity is the nature of life. It's naturalistic theology that informs this and Santayana who proves that romanticizing fluidity is necessary for ideals to survive" (#35)
- "Philosopher Gödel would argue that belief structures by nature will always be disrupted. We shouldn't treat change as evil" (#35)

**Against Empathy Critiques**
- Refutes: "Empathy is irrational" / "weakens theological rigor" / "is selective and unreliable" / "God doesn't need our feelings" / "We shouldn't anthropomorphize the divine" (#28–32)
- "Empathy is biased because it is informed by our own experiences. Now imagine a God that is omniscient and argue that empathy dilutes his will" (#30)
