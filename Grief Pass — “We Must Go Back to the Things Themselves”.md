## Rough Timeline of the Falling‑Out: Retention → Impression → Protention
- 1916–1923: Retention / Denial
    - Heidegger is Husserl's assistant at Freiburg. “My dear friend” letters; Husserl calls Heidegger his “philosophical son.” Disagreements framed as refinements.
    - Language anchor: still within phenomenological vocabulary, optimistic that differences are productive.
    - Grief stage: denial — “We’re on the same project.”
- 1923–1927: Impression / Anger
    - Heidegger moves to Marburg. Being and Time (1927) dedicated to Husserl yet rejects his method.
    - Language anchor: aggressive tonal shift — “thrownness,” “falling,” *das Man*.
    - Grief stage: anger — dedication reads like a dare.
- 1928–1933: Bargaining
    - Heidegger succeeds Husserl at Freiburg; Husserl revises, speaks of “generative phenomenology.”
    - Language anchor: “sedimented,” “tradition,” “generative.”
    - Grief stage: bargaining — lifeworld as attempted integration.
- 1933–1936: Depression (The Crisis)
    - Heidegger becomes rector, joins Nazi party; Husserl banned from university; library access revoked. Writes The Crisis.
    - Language anchor: “crisis,” “spiritual sickness,” “collapse.”
    - Grief stage: depression — personal betrayal turns political and existential.
- 1938: Acceptance / Protention
    - Husserl dies; Crisis appears posthumously.
    - Language anchor: *Lebenswelt* offered forward. Not for Heidegger anymore — for whoever comes after.
    - Grief stage: acceptance — something may yet grow.

## The Real Timeline: A Father Loses Everything Twice
- 1916: The first wound
    - Wolfgang Husserl (Edmund’s son) killed at Verdun. Husserl shattered. Heidegger already assistant.
    - Context for “philosophical son” language. Reduction as refuge; rigor as armor.
- 1919–1923: The replacement
    - Warm letters, paternal investment. Subtext: you’re all I have left.
- 1927: Being and Time — the second death
    - Dedication as cruelty. Vocabulary becomes visceral: thrown, abandoned; finitude as lived reality.
- 1928–1933: Bargaining with ghosts
    - Chair succession; generative phenomenology as “maybe if…”
- 1933: The third death (political)
    - Rectorate, party membership, bans; books burned. Personal becomes historical.
- 1936–1938: What remains
    - Writes Crisis. Lifeworld as final gift. Vocabulary narrows: crisis, world, life, together, remains.
