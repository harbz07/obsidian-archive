TRANSCRIPT:
The class covered developing countries' competitive strategies, focusing on factor input markets and capital markets. Discussion included how developing countries are granted privileges in competitive strategies, particularly relating to factor input markets and capital markets.
China's Economic Development:
- China has gone from a rounding error in GDP in the 1970s to the second largest economy
- 40 years of opening up policy marked today (from 1978)
- Deng Xiaoping's transformative reform launched December 18, 1978
- Moved from Soviet planned economy model to Western market economy
- GDP: $150 billion in 1978 → $1 trillion in 1997 → $12+ trillion recently

Developing Country Strategies:
1. Export standardized manufacturing (textiles, tires, simple electrical goods)
2. Low-cost assembly of technologically advanced products (e.g., iPhone assembly)
3. Low-tech manufacturing (toys, simple inputs)

Apple's Global Supply Chain:
- Most products assembled in China
- 2013 Mac Pro was exception - assembled in USA
- Complex global supply network across 25+ countries
- Austin, Texas plant had issues with local component sourcing
- Trade war complications and tariff considerations
- Moving some production to India and Vietnam

Commodity Markets & Cartels:
- Long-term commodity prices trend downward
- Factors depressing prices: Engel's law (income elasticity <1), synthetic substitutes, demand shifts
- Factors raising prices: Nature's limits, slow productivity growth in primary sector

OPEC & Cartel Economics:
- Cartels constrain supply to raise prices
- Model: Set output where marginal revenue = marginal cost
- Example: Market equilibrium at $40/barrel (40 million barrels/day) vs cartel price $100/barrel (30 million barrels/day)
- Challenges: Sagging demand, new competing supply (fracking), member countries cheating on agreements

Strategic Petroleum Reserve (SPR):
- Biden's 2022 oil trading strategy
- Released 180 million barrels when prices were ~$95/barrel
- Began refilling at $67/barrel in July 2023
- Generated ~$582 million profit on replaced barrels
- Created price stability and weakened OPEC's cartel power
- Positioned US as both seller of last resort (at market tops) and buyer of last resort (at market bottoms)
