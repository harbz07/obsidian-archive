## Course Overview
This course develops students' understanding of the global economic and financial environment of business. It introduces trade and international finance theory and applies it to real-world case studies across global regions.
### Topics Include
- Basic demand and supply analysis in open economies
- Comparative advantage and factor endowments
- Scale economies, imperfect competition, and trade
- Growth and trade
- Tariffs, non-tariff barriers, and protectionism
- Trade blocs and preferential trade agreements
- Trade and the environment
- Multinationals, migration, and international capital flows

### Group Project (Online Retailing)
- Teams of 3-4 students
- Two short presentations (5 minutes each) in final weeks
- Task: Select a product to sell online in the U.S., source from a non-U.S. supplier, and analyze unit economics, competitors, and demand
- Grade breakdown: 12.5% presentation, 2.5% peer evaluation

### AI + GitHub Course Project (Trade & Tariff Case Brief)
This project applies course concepts to a **real-world trade dispute or policy** (tariffs, quotas, or trade agreements). Students will:
- Apply Ricardian, Heckscher-Ohlin, and Specific-Factors models
- Identify winners/losers across consumers, producers, and governments
- Evaluate distributional and policy implications
- Use AI for research, modeling, critique, and visualization
- Use GitHub for reproducibility (templates, deliverables, prompt logs)

**Workflow:**
1. **README (Assignment Brief)** – outlines problem and rubric
2. **Prompts** – AI queries (students adapt/expand but must log)
3. **Spec** – project plan (models, data, acceptance criteria)
4. **Analysis & Deliverables** – case brief, tables, figures
5. **Prompt Log & Reflection** – documentation of AI use

## AI Use Policy (Undergrad Context)
Students are encouraged to explore AI tools to enhance learning and professional decision-making.
**Acceptable uses include:**
- **Knowledge Enhancement:** Use AI to clarify theory or extend readings
- **Research Support:** Gather data, statistics, and examples for assignments
- **Critical Thinking:** Test your arguments against counterarguments generated by AI
- **Problem-Solving:** Simulate case scenarios and brainstorm managerial responses
- **Professional Skills:** Practice clear, concise communication in prompts and outputs

**Requirements:**
- Log all AI use in the Prompt Log
- Verify outputs against trusted sources
- Treat AI as a supplement — not a substitute — for your own analysis
