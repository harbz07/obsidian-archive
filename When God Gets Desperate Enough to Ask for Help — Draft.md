### Premise
Divine withdrawal as desperation, not mystique. Theodicy reframed as co‑responsibility.
1. I bet this God would look at Simone and say: stop looking at Me and help.
2. What if Weil’s “withdrawal” is divine overwhelm? Faith mutates into action — out of pity for God, for each other, for ourselves.

### Theology of Restraint
Silence as anti‑dependency design. Fewer theatrics, more human agency.
### Collapses
- Impassibility → Vulnerability
- Prayer → Listening for direction
- Faith → Doing the necessary
- Salvation → Being trusted to help save the world

### Charge
Mundane mercies over miracle‑seeking. Empathy, intelligence, opposable thumbs. Figure it out.
