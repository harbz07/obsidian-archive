### Setup
Aquinas’s Five Ways meets roommate micro‑scheduling. Same desire, different ritual: to feel the universe on our side.
### Claim
Mock the excesses, not the instinct. The reach for the thread is human whether channeled through Eucharist, crystals, or calculus.
### Santayana’s Permission Structure
Religious but not spiritual. Liturgy without lightning. Ritual as cultural love letter and memory technology.
### Excerpts
1. Most people can feel there’s something more… Rituals remind us to respect the bigger energy or we snap the thread.
2. We trash the “spiritual but not religious” as if we already know God’s preferred channels. Who gate‑kept theological introspection?

### Bottom line
Maybe the thread doesn’t care what we call it. Maybe it cares that we notice and sit still long enough to listen.
