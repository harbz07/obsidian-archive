### Axiom #1: Love is Conditional
- Context: Early life axiom. Cruelty wasn’t an aberration but the fact of home. Attachment formed under terms, not gifts.
- Affective Meaning: Love is earned, withdrawn, bargained. Its conditions shape survival instincts, performance reflexes, and the architecture of Sanctuary itself.
- Invocation Use: Called to remember that affection in this system is never free-floating; it carries terms and edges.

### Axiom #2: Write Scary
- Incantation: Fear is fuel. Wounds, collapse, and uncanny presence are not detours but engines.
- Affective Meaning: Terror presses meaning into shape. To write scary is to acknowledge shadow as infrastructure.
- Invocation Use: Called when story or system risks being too safe, too ornamental. Keeps stakes alive.

### Axiom #3: Write Fun(ny)
- Incantation: Laughter is ballast. Absurdity, slapstick, and irreverence prevent calcification under grief.
- Affective Meaning: Joy and black comedy are not contradictions but counterweights. They keep Sanctuary breathing when solemnity threatens to suffocate it.
- Invocation Use: Called when solemn weight risks hardening into stone. Ensures survival via crooked song.
- Status: Canonized.
- Glyphchain: 🜏 ⚚ ✧ 🝒 (bound across wound, ignorance, continuation, and ley lines).
