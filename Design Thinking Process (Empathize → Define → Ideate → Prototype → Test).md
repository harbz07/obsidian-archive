Summary: Canonical five-stage process (Stanford d.school) that balances divergence and convergence through empathy, creativity, and experimentation.
Purpose: Provide a holistic, iterative framework for human-centered problem solving.
Design Phase:
  - "[[Notion/Design Thinking Agent/Design Thinking Stages/Empathize|Empathize]]"
  - "[[Notion/Design Thinking Agent/Design Thinking Stages/Define|Define]]"
  - "[[Notion/Design Thinking Agent/Design Thinking Stages/Ideate|Ideate]]"
  - "[[Notion/Design Thinking Agent/Design Thinking Stages/Prototype|Prototype]]"
  - "[[Notion/Design Thinking Agent/Design Thinking Stages/Test|Test]]"
Pitfalls: Treating stages as linear; skipping empathy or testing under pressure.
Prompts: |-
  • “What have you learned about your user’s needs?”
  • “What prototype could help you learn fastest?”
  • “Where are we in the cycle?”
  • “Have we learned enough to move forward?”
Outputs / Artifacts: Process roadmap, progress tracker, reflection summary.
Use Case:
  - Process Coaching
  - Design Education
  - Workshop Facilitation
  - Team Alignment
  - Project Planning
Best For: Framing and navigating end-to-end design projects or workshops.
Teams & Stakeholders: Cross-functional teams, facilitators, educators.
Steps / Structure: |-
  • Use: Foundation for process navigation or reflection checkpoints.
  • Steps: 
      1. Empathize – understand users.
      2. Define – frame the problem.
      3. Ideate – generate ideas.
      4. Prototype – make to learn.
      5. Test – validate and refine.
