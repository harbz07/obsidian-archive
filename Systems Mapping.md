Type:
  - Strategy & Systems Thinking
Summary: Visual tool that helps teams see beyond linear cause-and-effect to understand how elements interact dynamically.
Purpose: Reveal interconnections, feedback loops, and leverage points across an ecosystem.
Design Phase:
  - "[[Notion/Design Thinking Agent/Design Thinking Stages/Reflect|Reflect]]"
Pitfalls: Mapping too many elements; mistaking correlation for causation; no follow-through.
Prompts: |-
  • “What’s influencing what?”
  • “Where are the feedback loops?”
  • “Who gains or loses from this change?”
Outputs / Artifacts: Systems map diagram, causal-loop chart, leverage-point notes.
Use Case:
  - Ecosystem Mapping
  - Systems Thinking
  - Leverage Point Identification
  - Root Cause Analysis
Best For: Complex service challenges, policy design, org or ecosystem mapping.
Teams & Stakeholders: Strategists, service designers, ops leaders, sustainability teams.
Steps / Structure: |-
  1. List key actors/elements.
  2. Draw connections (material, information, influence).
  3. Identify reinforcing vs. balancing loops.
  4. Spot leverage points for intervention.
