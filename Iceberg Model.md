Type:
  - Strategy & Systems Thinking
Summary: "Framework to diagnose root causes by examining four layers: events, patterns, structures, and underlying beliefs."
Purpose: Uncover the hidden structures and mental models driving surface events.
Design Phase:
  - "[[Notion/Design Thinking Agent/Design Thinking Stages/Reflect|Reflect]]"
Pitfalls: Stopping at surface analysis; failing to test mental-model assumptions.
Prompts: |-
  • “What deeper pattern do we see here?”
  • “What assumptions hold this in place?”
Outputs / Artifacts: Iceberg diagram with insights per layer.
Use Case:
  - Root Cause Analysis
  - Pattern Recognition
  - Systems Thinking
  - Learning Capture
  - Idea Remixing
Best For: Systems analysis, retrospectives, behavioral design.
Teams & Stakeholders: Designers, org development, researchers, leadership.
Steps / Structure: |-
  • Levels: Events → Patterns → Structures → Mental Models
  • Use: Diagnose root causes and hidden assumptions.
  • Steps:
      1. Describe the Event (what happened).
      2. Identify Patterns/Trends.
      3. Map Structures enabling them.
      4. Surface Mental Models sustaining them.
