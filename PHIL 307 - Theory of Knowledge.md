## Course Description
All other disciplines give us knowledge about some aspect of reality or some dimensions of our lives. The theory of knowledge or **epistemology**, however, gives us a deeper understanding of what 'knowledge' is – knowledge about *knowledge* itself.
In answering the basic question 'What is knowledge?' epistemologists have focused on a host of related issues: How is *knowing* different from mere *believing*? What is it to be *justified* or have *evidence* for our beliefs? What is the *extent* of human knowledge and can we not reasonable *doubt* what we claim to know?
Over and above traditional queries about how we come to make knowledge claims is, a concern that is very relevant these days: Why do conspiracy theories not give us knowledge and what underlies our attraction to 'fake news'? What if any, is the *political* significance of such questions? Are theories of knowing linked to social injustices and oppressions? In other words, can (re)thinking what we mean by 'knowledge' lead to a more *just* society?
## Course Objectives
- Expose students to debates and questions in the discipline of *epistemology*
- Familiarize them with some key *concepts* in Western theory of knowledge
- Introduce them to *close readings* of philosophical texts
- Enable them to discern and assess *arguments/presuppositions* in support of a view
- Initiate *writing* clear expositions, analyses and philosophical critiques

## Assessment
### 1. Two Reaction Papers (60%)
**6-7 double spaced pages each: 30 + 30 points**
Prompts will be given by Instructor and discussed in class. Critical examination of a topic covered in class with close reference to the readings. You will have about 7 days to complete the assignment. **Please submit paper copies.**
### 2. Four (Very) Short In-Class Quizzes (20%)
**4 × 5 points**
Short exercises (15 min) - You will be asked to write about one paragraph. The point here is to see if you are keeping up with your readings and the lectures and are *thinking* about the material. These exercises will ask you to relate the concepts we have discussed to each other and to lived experiences.
These will be a mix of in-class pop quizzes and take home exercises. **NO MAKE UP.** But I will give at least 5 or 6 and the highest 4 scores will be counted.
### 3. Reflection Paper (15%)
**5 pages: 15 points**
This assignment is the last for the class and is due on Dec 12. It will be a longer version of the Quiz – something in-between a class quiz and a paper.
**Due Friday, December 12.**
### 4. Class Participation (5%)
**5 points**
You are expected to share your understanding of the readings with the class and engage in respectful discussion.
