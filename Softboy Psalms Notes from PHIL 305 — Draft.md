### Overview
A semester in PHIL 305 turned a reflective journal into Softboy Psalms: theological hot takes meeting real grief, doubt, and fury at weaponized faith.
### Excerpts
1. There is no gospel worth preaching if it cannot sit quietly in a room full of people grieving and say nothing but: “I’m here.”
2. When my grandmother died and was rolled out of our family home, there was a silence. It broke in shared celebration and grief. Then an aunt toasted repentance and hell, and I remembered why murder happens at that point.
3. Augustine’s theology turns God into a cosmic judge who cannot abide imperfection. Original sin, predestination, total depravity — doctrines whose shadow falls into homes, hospitals, and silences.

### Thread
When classroom theology meets kitchen-table reality, pensées become psalms. Between Pascal, Weil, grief, and institutional bullshit, the work is to reconcile beautiful ideas with human tenderness.
