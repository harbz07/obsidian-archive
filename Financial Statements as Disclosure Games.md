### Anchor
Financial statements are disclosure games that reduce information asymmetry between insiders and outsiders.
### Key players and incentives
- Managers: want favorable perceptions while avoiding legal risk
- Investors and creditors: demand credible, comparable signals
- Auditors and regulators: raise cost of misreporting

### Signals
- Quality of earnings, cash conversion, conservative revenue recognition
- Ratio clusters: ROE, margins, turnover, leverage

### Links
- Upstream: Corporate structures and governance
- Sibling: Ratio analysis as screening
- Downstream: Cost of capital and funding access

### Example vs counterexample
- Example: Rising revenue with flat CFO → possible aggressive accruals
- Counterexample: Low net income but strong CFO due to depreciation → not distress

### Common trap → Fix
- Trap: Treating all earnings growth as equal
- Fix: Prioritize cash-based metrics and footnote changes
