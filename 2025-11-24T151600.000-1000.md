While I myself am not qualified to speak as an objective source of insight for the woman’s experience, I want to try and examine what I’ve heard and read from them to try and see if I can get somewhere that’s resonant. I feel like a lot of Feminist Philosophy resents a lack of reciprocity that becomes given in a Patriarchal Society.

I had a classmate in my Critical Theory and Marxism class that had a really interesting standpoint on the value of women’s labor, that they are directly responsible for creating the primitive inputs that circulates throughout our modes of production: workers. They give birth, yet they don’t get returned the value because they don’t get paid for it—to take a purely Marxist lens to the issue.

Taking note from Iris Marion Young, I think one of her points is that her existence as a woman necessarily needs to move in reaction to the masculine physicality around and about her. Not just that, but the way they’re seen is purely as objects, yet they themselves are subjects to their own islands. And their subjectivity itself is largely self-reliant. It’s like they’re alone in their sureness of their subjectivity. The woman needs to be realized as a subject of their own if we want to take a critical stance on the feminist standpoint. And to be more precise, we need to start recognizing that women are subjects of their own. We do not owe them our attention, we owe them our consideration.

I am a strong proponent of the concept of our metaphysical entrenchment in the social circumstances and dynamics that we exist in and I believe that solidarity is of paramount importance in egalitarian structures. I did a case study on the Girl Scouts of America and it became increasingly salient to me as I did my research that girls were very restricted in avenues to examine and build the idea of girlhood in a safe, open space—and even necessarily, with other girls. Women’s self-definition is an incredibly important source of information.

Women’s success will always be discredited, it’s how a patriarchal system works.
